SOUL EATER

[putting us together & forever]

In a chilling breakthrough at the intersection of neuroscience and technology, researchers have discovered a way to “consume” the consciousness of deceased loved ones, effectively integrating fragments of their minds into the living. Dubbed "SOUL EATER" by both scientists and skeptics, the process involves scanning and uploading neural imprints from the recently deceased into an advanced cognitive framework, allowing the memories, emotions, and even personalities of the departed to be accessed by those left behind. While hailed as a revolutionary step toward digital immortality, ethical concerns loom large, with critics questioning whether this is an act of remembrance or a horrifying distortion of identity.

The technology itself that are hauntingly familiar of Cyberpunk 2077’s concept of engram storage, blurs the line between life and death, pushing humanity into an era where grief and possession intertwine. Some corporations have already begun eyeing the potential of selling "soul imprints" as a means of eternal companionship, sparking fierce debates on consent, autonomy, and the commodification of human existence. As society wrestles with the implications, one thing is clear—this scientific advancement forces us to reconsider what it truly means to be alive, and more disturbingly, whether the dead can ever truly rest.
